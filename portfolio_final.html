About
Ray Olguin is a Research Engineering Technician with the High Resolution Imaging Science Experiment (HiRISE) at the University of Arizona's Lunar and Planetary Laboratory (LPL), a world-leading center for planetary science and space mission research. He supports Science Operations at HiROC — the U.S. facility for NASA's HiRISE instrument on the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO) — leading the HiRISE XR Development Program, producing HiRISE and CTX DTMs, and contributing to validation of planetary datasets for NASA's PDS in collaboration with senior planetary scientists at the PSI.

Pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering (EE) — Sophomore with minors in Planetary Science, Mathematics, and Computer Engineering at the University of Arizona. Combined training in project management, architectural & landscape design, XR simulations, and 3D visualization positions him at the intersection of satellite systems engineering, planetary science, and applied engineering. Pursuing an Accelerated M.S. in EE with a planetary science instrumentation focus, emphasizing electrical systems engineering, electronics design, and hardware integration for flight instruments.

Member: Wildcat Rocket Engineering Club · IEEE · AIAA. Goal: contribute to and ultimately lead large-scale planetary exploration programs, advancing U.S. leadership in space science hardware while enabling the exploration of new worlds across the solar system and beyond.
